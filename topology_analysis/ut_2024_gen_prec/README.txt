## Notes on Field Names (adapted from VEST):
Columns reporting votes generally follow the pattern: 
One example is:
G16PREDCLI
The first character is G for a general election, P for a primary, S for a special, and R for a runoff.
Characters 2 and 3 are the year of the election.*
Characters 4-6 represent the office type (see list below).
Character 7 represents the party of the candidate.
Characters 8-10 are the first three letters of the candidate's last name.

*To fit within the GIS 10 character limit for field names, the naming convention is slightly different for the State Legislature and US House of Representatives. All fields are listed below with definitions.

Office Codes Used:
PRE - US President
GOV - Governor
SAC - Court of Appeals Judge
SSC - Supreme Court Justice 
ATG - Attorney General
AUD - Auditor
TRE - Treasurer
SL## - State House of Representatives ##
SU## - State Senate ##
CON## - US House of Representatives ##
AMDX - Constitutional Amendment X (where X is the letter)

Party Codes Used:
C - Constitution
D - Democratic
F - Utah Forward
G - Green
I - Independent American
L - Libertarian
N - No Party / Nonpartisan
O - Other / Write In Votes
R - Republican
U - United Utah

## Processing Steps

Election data were retrieved from the source above as a JSON. The data were processed in python. Contests/candidates without a party, or where the party is listed as "UNKNOWN" or "UNAFFILIATED" are listed as "NONPARTISAN"/"NO PARTY" for the purposes of correctly grouping and pivoting results, as counties within the state report the parties differently.

Results for statewide contests and state legislative and congressional contests were queried out. Columns were renamed according to RDH standardization. Columns are organized by greatest votes within a contest.

Before merging returns with shapes, precincts in election results with no votes for any contest were removed. There are three counties which had absentee votes to allocate: Tooele, Utah, and Washington. Absentee votes are allocated based on a candidate's election day performance in a precinct. The formula for allocating votes is: (# of votes for candidate on election day in precinct / # of votes for candidate on election day in county) * # of absentee votes for candidate in county. A Hamilton rounding method is performed to ensure county totals for votes match exactly before and after the allocation of absentee votes. For more information on the method, see [here](https://redistrictingdatahub.org/vote-allocation/). 

Counties in the precinct file contain a CountyID that is not the FIPS. The fips is retrieved from joining with a county shapefile from the state where the County ID and FIPS are both included. Several shapes do not have county information, but from visual inspection are in Cache, and are therefore assigned to that county. In some counties, the VistaID (a more specific unit) corresponds to the election returns, while in others it corresponds to the PrecinctID. The majority correspond to the VistaID, but Daggett, Emery, Garfield, Iron, Kane, Millard, Piute, Sevier, Uintah, and Wayne correspond best to the PrecinctID, therefore geographies are dissolved on this field in those instances. 

The following counites were renamed from their shorter name as reported in the shapes to their longer names as reported in the election results (those mapping to multiple precincts are further noted in parentheses): Beaver (MV01:1 and MV01:2 also both mapped to Minersville 1), Box Elder (ELWD and ELWD:M both mapped to Elwood), Carbon, Cache, Emery, Garfield (B02 and BO2 both mapped to Boulder 02), Juab, Millard, Morgan (1:1 and 1:1-S to 1-1, 2:1 and 2:1-S to 2-1, 3:1 and 3:1-S to 3-1, 5:1 and 5:1-S to 5-1), Rich, Sanpete, Sevier, Uintah, Wasatch, Wayne, and Weber, Daggett, and Piute. 

There are 51 additional shapes that do not have a corresponding election return. They are no population/vote precincts, as confirmed by an L2 geocoded voterfile and joined to the election results and filled all contest returns to 0.

The file was cleaned using the [maup library](https://github.com/mggg/maup) to mitigate holes and overlap between shapes.

After joining the precincts to their boundaries, the files were checked for precinct-district splits. Ultimately, the precinct boundary and election result files are split across four different files. The "_all_" file contains all the election results joined to precinct boundaries, but does not account for the various precinct-district splits identified in processing and as such, does not contain district assignments. The "_cong_", "_sldl_", and "_sldu_" files contain Congressional, State House and State Senate results at the most granular units for which data is available. Please note that in order to minimize superfluous splits, precinct splits are only made when a precinct contains votes for candidates in multiple districts, as such district assignments for precincts many not perfectly correspond to the districts themselves, particularly in large, uninhabited areas.

## Additional Notes

In some contests, the results do not sum perfectly to the totals that are reported by Utah. That is because in the precinct returns "some results have been hidden in cases where not enough ballots were cast to ensure voter privacy." These represent very few votes across the state.
